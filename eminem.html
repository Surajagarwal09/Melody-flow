EMINEM


Marshall Bruce Mathers III 


  Marshall Bruce Mathers III, known professionally as Eminem, the influential American rapper, broke racial
  barriers for white artists in hip-hop. His controversial work resonated with the American underclass and
  influenced artists across genres. Notable albums: The Slim Shady LP, The Marshall Mathers LP, and 
  The Eminem Show. Iconic songs: “Lose Yourself,” “Stan,” “Not Afraid,” and “Rap God.”
  Achievements: Grammy Awards, Billboard hits, and an Academy Award for “Lose Yourself.”
  Eminem’s impact extends beyond music, including film appearances and his own radio channel.
